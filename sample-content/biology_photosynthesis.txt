INTRODUCTION
============
Photosynthesis is the fundamental process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert light energy from the sun into chemical energy stored in glucose (sugar). This process is the foundation of almost all life on Earth, as it produces the oxygen we breathe and the food energy that flows through ecosystems.

THE PHOTOSYNTHESIS EQUATION
============================
The overall chemical equation for photosynthesis is:

  6CO‚āā  +  6H‚āāO  +  Light Energy  ‚Üí  C‚āÜH‚āĀ‚āāO‚āÜ  +  6O‚āā
(Carbon Dioxide + Water + Sunlight ‚Üí Glucose + Oxygen)

WHERE DOES PHOTOSYNTHESIS OCCUR?
==================================
Photosynthesis happens inside the CHLOROPLAST, a specialized organelle found in plant cells.

Structure of a Chloroplast:
  [OUTER MEMBRANE] - Outer protective layer
  [INNER MEMBRANE] - Inner barrier
  [THYLAKOIDS]     - Flat, disc-shaped sacs arranged in stacks called GRANA
                     ‚Üí This is where the LIGHT-DEPENDENT REACTIONS happen
                     ‚Üí Contains CHLOROPHYLL (the green pigment that absorbs light)
  [STROMA]         - Fluid surrounding the thylakoids
                     ‚Üí This is where the LIGHT-INDEPENDENT REACTIONS (Calvin Cycle) happen

THE TWO STAGES OF PHOTOSYNTHESIS
==================================

STAGE 1: LIGHT-DEPENDENT REACTIONS (in the Thylakoids)
  Step 1: Chlorophyll absorbs sunlight (mainly red and blue light)
  Step 2: Water molecules are split (PHOTOLYSIS): 2H‚āāO ‚Üí 4H‚Āļ + 4e‚ĀĽ + O‚āā
  Step 3: Oxygen is released as a by-product (this is the O‚āā we breathe!)
  Step 4: Energy is captured as ATP and NADPH - these are energy carrier molecules

STAGE 2: LIGHT-INDEPENDENT REACTIONS - CALVIN CYCLE (in the Stroma)
  Step 1: CO‚āā from the air enters and is "fixed" by an enzyme called RuBisCO
  Step 2: CO‚āā combines with a 5-carbon molecule (RuBP) to form a 6-carbon compound
  Step 3: ATP and NADPH from Stage 1 are used to convert this into G3P
  Step 4: G3P is used to make glucose and to regenerate RuBP
  Step 5: The cycle continues...

FACTORS AFFECTING PHOTOSYNTHESIS
===================================
1. LIGHT INTENSITY  ‚Üí More light = faster photosynthesis (up to a maximum)
2. CARBON DIOXIDE   ‚Üí More CO‚āā = faster photosynthesis (up to a maximum)
3. TEMPERATURE      ‚Üí Optimum temperature: 25-35¬įC. Too hot or cold slows it down.
4. WATER AVAILABILITY ‚Üí Without water, photosynthesis cannot occur
5. CHLOROPHYLL      ‚Üí More chlorophyll allows more light absorption

KEY VOCABULARY
===============
- Chloroplast: Organelle where photosynthesis occurs
- Chlorophyll: Green pigment that absorbs sunlight
- Thylakoid: Membrane structure where light reactions occur
- Stroma: Fluid where Calvin Cycle occurs
- ATP: Adenosine triphosphate - the cell's energy currency
- Calvin Cycle: Series of reactions that fix CO‚āā into glucose
- Photolysis: Splitting of water molecules using light energy
- RuBisCO: Enzyme that fixes CO‚āā in the Calvin Cycle
